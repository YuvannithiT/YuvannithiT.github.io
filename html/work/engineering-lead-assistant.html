Engineering Lead Assistant

Eagle Flight Research Center | May 2020 - August 2020

Responsibilities

  • Created a 4-DOF Lagrangian dynamics model of a powered parachute using MATLAB and Simulink.
  • Enhanced simulation accuracy by integrating flight data, reducing computational error margins by 25%.
  • Collaborated with teams on failure mode analysis and proposed designs aligning with aerospace standards.

Experience Breakdown

During my tenure as an Engineering Lead Assistant at the Eagle Flight Research Center in Embry-Riddle, I played a pivotal role in developing an enhanced and reliable predictive model for a powered-parachute aircraft (PPC) to be used for military support roles and civil-military applications. Below are the key highlights of my responsibilities and achievements:

  • Led a team of two engineers to model the longitudinal behavior of the PPC, achieving a 15% improvement in predictive accuracy compared to previous models.
  • Developed a deep understanding of flight mechanics, parachute aerodynamics, and model-based simulation techniques.
  • Collaborated with cross-functional teams to identify design issues and propose changes adhering to FAA and industry standards.
  • Maintained a continuous work loop involving clients, technicians, and my team, fostering a culture of innovation and improvement.

To achieve these results, I utilized advanced tools and techniques, which included:

  • Developing and validating a 4-degree-of-freedom (4-DOF) Lagrangian-based dynamics model using MATLAB and Simulink to simulate the longitudinal behavior of the PPC.
  • Integrating real-life full-scale aerodynamics data into the simulation model, improving reliability and reducing errors by 25%.

The outcomes of these efforts were significant:

  • Provided insights into the PPC’s performance under various conditions, informing design decisions and optimization strategies.
  • Identified weight reduction opportunities in the prototype, resulting in a 10% weight reduction, enhancing range and vertical climb profile performance.
  • Contributed to cost savings and operational efficiency through these optimizations.

This experience not only advanced the field of powered-parachute modeling but also deepened my understanding of aircraft dynamics. It honed my technical skills in MATLAB, Simulink, and Git, while enhancing soft skills such as technical communication, leadership, and problem-solving in a dynamic engineering environment.
